Cookies

Sometimes a “cookie” file may be stored on the device you use to access the Website and its associated applications and services in order to improve your user experience. A cookie is a small piece of data sent to your device from a website and stored in your web browser while you are browsing a website. When you return to the website and browse, the data stored in the cookie may be retrieved by the website to notify the website of your previous activity.

Most browsers are set to accept cookies and you can change your settings to refuse all cookies or alert you when a cookie is being sent. If you visit our Website and your browser settings accept cookies, we consider this as an express consent to our use of cookies. To read more about cookies and how they work, kindly visit http://www.allaboutcookies.org.

Why do we use cookies?

Certain cookies are necessary to run basic functions on the Website and its associated applications and services. These cookies are needed to provide you with the best user experience possible. We may also use cookies to provide better services and products to you. By using cookies, the Website and its associated applications and services may remember the language and country choices you have made during past visits. We may conduct analytical profiling to learn what services and web pages are popular among visitors. We also determine where our content is accessed to structure our site for the best possible user experience. In addition, we count clicks on social media sharing buttons to analyse the content of the Website and its associated applications and services that have been shared or referenced.

Third-party cookies

Third parties, such as advertisers, advertising networks and providers of tracking services, may set cookies on your device when you are visiting the Website and its associated applications and services, for example, to provide you with targeted advertising or to compile statistics on the number of visitors. Since your device must request these advertisements from third parties servers, these third parties may view, modify or set their own cookies as if you had requested a webpage from the third parties’ website directly. We do not control these third parties tracking technologies or how they may be used. You may prevent the placement of and tracking by such third-party cookies by adjusting or modifying the appropriate settings on your device.

Duration cookies are stored

The length of time that cookies remain on websites generally depends on the type of cookie used. Session cookies expire when the browser is closed, while persistent cookies have typical expiration dates between two months and a couple of years. We may decide to use any one of these or a combination of two or more.
